WCAG minimum rules: Use Brand 600+ for body text on white (min 4.5:1 AA). Use Brand 400–500 only for large text (18px+), icons, and borders (min 3:1). Use 50–300 exclusively for surface fills, chips, and hover states — never as text. The same rules apply to your secondary scale.

03 · Foundation

Spacing & Grid

4px base unit (Material Design) or 8pt base (Apple HIG). Every spacing value is a multiple of the base unit. Toggle above to switch systems.

4px base grid (Material)
4px grid (Material) — More granular. 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 32, 48, 64. Optimal for data-dense UIs, dashboards, and enterprise products.

8pt grid (Apple HIG) — Fewer values, larger jumps. 8, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, 64. Optimal for consumer apps, marketing sites, and products following iOS/macOS conventions.

18 · Dark Mode

Dark Surfaces

Two dark modes — toggle between them using the sidebar. Brand-tinted surfaces keep your brand identity in dark mode. Neutral grey is the industry standard used by GitHub, VS Code, Linear, and Vercel.

Brand-tinted surfaces — generated from your brand hue

When to use: Products where brand identity is central — consumer apps, marketing tools, creative platforms. The brand color bleeds into every surface.

Neutral dark grey scale — used by GitHub, VS Code, Linear, Vercel

When to use: Tools, dashboards, developer products, content-heavy apps. Neutral grey lets the content lead — brand color only appears on interactive elements, not surfaces. This is the most widely adopted dark mode pattern.
